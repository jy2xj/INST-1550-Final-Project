The History of Racism

The first signs of a racist view of the world could have appeared during the 13th and 14th centuries with the identification of the jews with the devil and witchcraft in the popular mind of the time. During the 16th century was when Jews who had converted to Christianity became the victims of a pattern of discrimination and exclusion.

During the Renaissance and Reformation periods was when Europeans came into greater contact with people of darker skin color. The original justification for enslaving Africans was that they were heathens. But in 1667, when Virginia decreed that converted slaves could be kept in bondage for heathen ancestry and not because they were actual heathens, the reason for slavery was changed from religious status to something approaching race.
