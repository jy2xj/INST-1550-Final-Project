Racial Tensions Grow at University of Kansas

A section that elaborates on recent events

At the University of Kansas, there has been growing racial tensions, which has led to a call for three student Senate leaders to step down. The Senate's Student Executive Committee registered a 6 to 3 "no-confidence" vote for the three leaders on Friday. What is a "no-confidence" vote? It is a vote that finds that someone is not fit to hold their position, due to various reasons. The three leaders: the student body president, vice president, and chief staff, have been demanded by the committee to resign or face imminent impeachment.



This resignation demand stems from weeks of unrest at the University of Missouri, with protests that started after a series of racist events that students said administrators were not taking seriously. These protests eventually lead to the resignation of University of Missouri President Tim Wolfe. A forum was also moderated at KU where the student group Rock Chalk Invisible Hawk presented diversity demands. Some of the demands include increased diversity in hiring and "inclusion and belonging" training for students and faculty.

A 2014 KU graduate also came back to support the cause. He began a campus strike in solidarity with KU student groups.
